Onboarding Policy

Rules and responsibilities for society registration, subscription activation, tenant id creation, and blocks/flats setup.

Society registration Subscription selection Tenant id creation Blocks and flats setup
Page 1

Society Registration Terms

The society admin must provide accurate community name, address, contact person, email, phone number, and registration details. SmartSociety may verify submitted information before activating the tenant workspace.

The registering user confirms that they are authorized by the apartment association, builder, management committee, or property owner group to create a digital workspace.

False, duplicate, or misleading registration requests may be rejected, suspended, or merged after platform review.

Page 2

Subscription Selection Terms

Plan features, user limits, flat limits, storage, billing modules, visitor modules, reports, and payment features are controlled by the selected subscription.

Plan upgrades may activate additional modules immediately. Downgrades may restrict features after the current billing cycle ends.

SmartSociety may notify the admin before restricting access to premium modules when a plan expires or payment fails.

Page 3

Tenant ID Creation Terms

Each registered society receives a unique tenant id. This tenant id separates residents, flats, bills, visitors, complaints, expenses, and reports from other societies.

Tenant id values must not be manually reused across different communities. Data isolation is mandatory for all SmartSociety operations.

Super admins may audit tenant metadata for security, compliance, support, and billing reasons.

Page 4

Blocks and Flats Setup Terms

The society admin is responsible for adding correct block names, flat numbers, ownership type, resident mapping, and occupancy status.

Incorrect flat mapping may affect billing, visitor approvals, complaint assignment, and resident access permissions.

Bulk imports must be reviewed before inviting residents to avoid sending access links to the wrong user.
